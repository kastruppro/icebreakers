# Milo Swiftclock - Full Backstory

## Childhood & The Luxon Fragment

Milo Swiftclock was born in the bustling streets of [Neverwinter](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Neverwinter)'s [Protector's Enclave](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Protector%27s_Enclave). His father, **[[Ren_Swiftclock|Ren Swiftclock]]**, operates the city's most prestigious horology workshop, catering to nobles, guildmasters, and officials, while his mother, **[[Serina_Swiftclock|Serina Swiftclock]]**, manages the storefront with welcoming professionalism.

From the moment he could wind a mainspring, Milo was enthralled by the tick-tock precision of clocks - and by the invisible force they measured. Endless evenings were spent in the workshop attic, diagramming gear ratios and sketching spell-circles side-by-side.

During one of those attic rambles, Milo uncovered a false panel in an heirloom chest that belonged to Serina. Inside lay a palm-sized sliver of crystalline amber that thrummed with faint, rhythmic pulses: a **[Luxon](https://criticalrole.fandom.com/wiki/Luxon) beacon fragment**. Startled but spellbound, Milo kept the shard secret, suspecting it was a relic from his mother's adventuring days - stories she never quite finished telling.

## The Break with Gearwhistle

Months later he could no longer contain his curiosity. He brought the fragment to his mentor, **[[Master_Tiberius_Gearwhistle|Master Tiberius Gearwhistle]]**, hoping the gnome's genius would help him stabilise and study it. Gearwhistle's reaction was swift and furious: the Luxon's power was too volatile, the source too dubious. He ordered Milo to return the fragment and drop the matter.

Convinced he could unlock dunamantic breakthroughs, Milo disobeyed. Gearwhistle eventually caught him mid-experiment, temporal echoes swirling around the workshop. The confrontation shattered their relationship. Gearwhistle informed his parents of the transgression. Feeling betrayed by master and family alike, Milo left home.

## The Pull Toward Sharandar

After the falling out with Gearwhistle, Milo needed to get out of Neverwinter. He didn't have a plan for where to go.

All [harengon](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Harengon) have a connection to the [Feywild](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Feywild) - they speak Sylvan, they carry fey traits in their blood. Most harengon growing up on the Material Plane don't think much about it. Milo always did. He'd had this restlessness since he was a kid, a pull he couldn't quite explain: his fur standing up near old forest, weird dreams about time folding in on itself, a seasonal unease that came and went. Looking back, [[Liora_Swiftclock|Liora]] probably had the same thing going on - hers just came out as prophetic dreams and wanting to run off and explore, while Milo's came out as an obsession with temporal magic. Neither of them knew at the time that their family had way more history with this kind of thing than their parents let on - Serina's old spellbook, their grandfather disappearing through a void portal, generations of secret cult research. That all came out later.

When [[Elira_Moonshadow|Elira Moonshadow]] told Milo about the [Sharandar](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Sharandar) restoration - the ruins in [Neverwinter Wood](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Neverwinter_Wood), the [eladrin](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Eladrin) who had come back from the Feywild, the fey crossing hidden in an old oak tree - he just knew he had to go there. Elira connected him with **[Iliyasha Guidingstar](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Iliyasha_Guidingstar)**, the sun elf ambassador who recruited help for [New Sharandar](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/New_Sharandar) from Protector's Enclave. The eladrin were generally suspicious of outsiders, but a harengon was different - fey-born, Sylvan-speaking. Not a typical Neverwinter local. Iliyasha gave him a shot.

## A Year at Sharandar

Milo ended up spending about a year at the restoration. The base of operations was a cluster of utilitarian wooden structures - log cabins and tree houses - built in and around the ruins of **[Old Sharandar](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Sharandar)**. Nothing fancy. The contrast was actually kind of depressing: you'd walk past the remains of theurglass furniture and crumbling carvings of [Corellon](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Corellon_Larethian) on your way to a plank cabin with a leaky roof. The **Portal Courtyard** - the fortified courtyard around the [fey crossing](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Fey_crossroad) - was nearby but off-limits to most workers. Milo could see the outer walls of living vegetation from where he slept.

He reported to **[Thradisar Starwise](https://neverwinter.fandom.com/wiki/Thradisar_Starwise)**, a wizard-merchant who ran the scholarly side of the restoration. Thradisar was sharp and didn't care about enthusiasm - but he figured out pretty fast that Milo's Luxon fragment was actually useful.

Most of Milo's actual work happened in the **Hidden Vaults** beneath Old Sharandar. A lot of these crypts - full of old [Iliyanbruen](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Iliyanbruen) artefacts, texts, holy icons - were sealed behind **stasis-wards**, temporal magic tied to the city's [mythal](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Mythal). Thousands of years old. The wards kept everything frozen in time, perfectly preserved, but they were also dangerous. Get a reading wrong and you could destroy what the ward was protecting, or get yourself stuck in a pocket of stopped time. The eladrin could feel the wards intuitively, but they couldn't measure them precisely enough to safely take them apart.

Milo's Luxon fragment resonated with the wards. When he got close, the shard would pulse in time with whatever temporal frequency the ward was running on. At first he tried to approach it the way he approached everything - diagrams, measurements, trying to map the temporal structure like a clock mechanism. It worked, kind of. He could get readings, identify which wards were stable and which were dangerous. But he kept hitting a wall when it came to actually unwinding them. The wards weren't mechanical - they were organic, layered, and they shifted in ways his models couldn't predict.

It was **[[Varek]]**, a [satyr](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Satyr) mason on the restoration crews, who accidentally gave him the breakthrough. Varek worked entirely by instinct - weight, rhythm, gut feeling. Everything Milo found infuriating about him. One day Varek watched Milo struggling with a ward for the third hour straight and told him to stop treating the ruins like a patient on a table and start listening to them like music. Milo hated the advice. But that night he went back to the vault and tried it - stopped measuring and just... listened to what the fragment was telling him. Felt the rhythm of the ward instead of trying to chart it. And it clicked. The shard wasn't giving him data points. It was giving him a feel for the temporal flow, the way a musician hears when something is off-key.

After that, Milo's ward-work got dramatically better. Under Thradisar's supervision he learned to read the resonances by feel - which parts were stable, which were about to collapse, and where to start unwinding. That's really how he learned to control his chronomancy. Not from books, and not from pure analysis either - from learning to trust what the fragment was showing him. Spells like *Chronal Shift* and *Momentary Stasis* came out of that practical experience. The horologist's precision was still there, but now it was guided by something more intuitive.

### Life Among the Eladrin

The eladrin didn't quite know what to do with him. Most of them were wary of outsiders - the ruins had been looted before, and a lot of eladrin still saw Neverwinter's people as squatters on their ancestral land. But Milo was a harengon, which meant he was technically from the Feywild even though he'd never been there. He spoke Sylvan, he had fey magic baked into his racial traits, and he was clearly drawn to the place in a way that wasn't just academic curiosity. Some of the eladrin found that a bit sad - a Feywild creature who'd never seen his own homeland. Others got it, especially the ones who remembered their own exile.

The fey crossing was right there - a massive oak with a hole in the trunk big enough for a wagon. The eladrin called it "the hole in the world." Milo never got permission to go through it, but he could feel it all the time. His Luxon shard reacted to it constantly. It felt like something he'd been looking for without knowing it.

The first few weeks were rough. [Neverwinter Wood](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Neverwinter_Wood) doesn't want you there - the [mythal](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Mythal) keeps the trees aware, and you can feel something watching you all the time. It gets into your head, especially at night. Most of the workers slept near a waterfall because the noise drowned it out enough to actually get some rest. Milo got used to it eventually, but those first weeks he barely slept at all.

### People at Sharandar

**[Merrisara Winterwhite](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Merrisara_Winterwhite)** commanded the whole operation. Milo didn't deal with her often, but she approved his access levels and once watched him work on a stasis-field around a cache of old [Iliyanbruen](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Iliyanbruen) blades without saying a word.

Varek stayed Milo's main working partner for the rest of his time there. They still annoyed each other constantly, but they got good results together. Milo brought precision, Varek brought instinct, and between them they opened more vaults than either could have managed alone.

**[Emrae Firesky](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Emrae_Firesky)**, the priestess of [Corellon](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Corellon_Larethian) at the **Temple of Leaves**, invited him to a **[Lateu'quor](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Lateu%27quor)** - the Forest Communion of the Crescent Moon. It's a monthly ceremony that Corellon's faithful hold during the first waxing crescent, always in a natural grove, never indoors. The worshippers bring offerings - artwork, crafted items, things they've made - and there's singing and dancing under the moonlight. The priests wear these azure gossamer robes with silver crescent moons embroidered on them. What got Milo was watching the eladrin shift between seasonal forms as they sang - one moment a summer aspect, the next autumn, their emotions visible and shared across the whole congregation. Milo follows Oghma, not Corellon, so he was there as a guest, but standing in a sacred grove surrounded by fey creatures worshipping their creator god under a crescent moon... that's the closest he's come to understanding what the Feywild actually is. He's thought about it more than he'd admit.

When the restoration work got close to the **[Mended Grove](https://neverwinter.fandom.com/wiki/The_Mended_Grove)** - an area that used to be called the Blighted Grove before the eladrin managed to restore it, but was still sketchy around the edges from old dark fey activity - Milo dealt with **[Pelendethas Everstride](https://neverwinter.fandom.com/wiki/Pelendethas_Everstride)**, the military coordinator. Pelendethas didn't care about Milo's research, but he liked having someone around who could tell him which wards were safe and which ones were live.

**[Cerandis Greenward](https://neverwinter.fandom.com/wiki/Cerandis_Greenward)**, the scout leader, taught Milo the practical stuff - safe paths through Neverwinter Wood, where the mythal's sentient trees were most active, and the basic rule near the crossing: respect the boundary, no matter how curious you are.

### The Mythal Incident

Near the end of his time there, Milo was working in a collapsed chamber under Old Sharandar when his Luxon shard hit a dormant mythal ward. The whole chamber shifted, his senses went sideways for a second, and he ended up standing a few paces from where he'd been with dust hanging motionless in the air. Nothing dramatic - he didn't go to the Feywild or anything - but the eladrin who saw it said *"the mythal has marked the hare."* After that he got access to some deeper records.

He would have stayed longer. He was getting close to maybe being allowed supervised access near the crossing. But Liora's letters got strange and then stopped coming entirely. So he left.

## Back in Neverwinter

When news arrived that **Liora had vanished**, Milo dropped everything and hurried back to Neverwinter. There he reunited with Serina, who pressed into his paws a hastily folded scrap of parchment - a cryptic note in Liora's own hand:

> *Do not trust the shadow,*
> *trust that I know best.*
> *I beg of you not to go look for me.*
> *If you find me, do not free me, for it will mean the end,*
> *the chained released*

Milo launched a frantic search through the city's dockside taverns, guild archives, and back-alley informants, chasing any flicker of his sister's trail. To finance the investigation Milo accepted a part-time post at Neverwinter's **[House of Knowledge](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Hall_of_Knowledge_(Neverwinter))** - the great cathedral-library of **[[Oghma]]**. His relentless enquiries about planar anomalies and Sharandar mythals soon attracted the wary attention of **Professor Thaddeus Blackthorn**, a visiting dunamancer who became convinced the young harengon might trigger a temporal catastrophe.

There, late one evening, Elira revealed that she served as an **Ink-Scribe of Oghma**. Recognising Milo's meticulous journals, she gifted him a feather quill of chalk-white plumage. The quill refills with radiant ink only when he commits *genuinely new* discoveries to parchment. Milo felt an almost sacred accord and quietly pledged his scholarship to Oghma's service.

## The Road to Phandalin

Rumours reached Neverwinter of **temporal anomalies** and bizarre weather around Phandalin - summer snow, orcs descending from the mountains, and centaurs roaming the woods - echoing Liora's prophetic dreams. Convinced the events were linked, Milo packed his Luxon-timepiece, the white quill, and a fast-drying supply of blank journals, and set out for Phandalin to find his sister and the truth.

It wasn't until later - finding the secret room in his parents' house, seeing his mother's old adventurer's spellbook, learning about grandfather Raas disappearing through a portal - that Milo realised the pull wasn't just his. The whole Swiftclock family had been tangled up in this stuff for generations. The difference was that Milo went to Sharandar to study the edges of the Feywild. Liora went looking for what was on the other side.
