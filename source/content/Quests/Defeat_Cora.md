# Defeat Cora

## Quick Reference
**Status:** On Tactical Hold (Session 31: ambush failed at the threshold; [[Cora]] now operating as intermediary to the Queens' court)
**Priority:** CRITICAL
**Quest Giver:** The Icebreakers (declared by [[Clover]] at end of Session 30)
**Started:** Session 30
**Last Updated:** Session 31

## Objective
Kill [[Cora]] -- crown princess of the Unseelie Court, daughter of the [[Queen of Darkness]], and the cult's most effective agent against the party. Do not destroy [[The Moonlit Pool]] site in the process.

## Background
[[Cora]] has been the party's recurring antagonist from Session 01. She kidnapped [[Liora_Swiftclock|Liora]] twice. She has a personal short-range flash-teleport (cleared a 100-foot [[Plant Growth]] in a single move in Session 29). She can open triangular portals. She has escaped the party in every prior encounter.

Session 30 changed the picture completely:
- [[Mr. Plant]] revealed that Cora is the **daughter of the Unseelie** -- the crown princess of the Winter Court.
- The [[Queen of Darkness]] (ruler of the Unseelie, Cora's mother) manifested in the party's path and threatened them personally.
- The party finally arrived at [[The Moonlit Pool]] in the Feywild and found Cora standing at the dry pool, her back to them, directing Clover's parents to "protect Okramok" and jump into the pool.
- The party is in the tree line. Cora does not know they are there (yet). She does know she was *"followed in here"* -- she said so aloud.

- **Constraints:**

  - Cora has flash-teleport -- she has escaped every prior encounter

## Session 31 - Ambush Failed at the Threshold, Cora Pivoted to Intermediary
The planned ambush at the [[The Moonlit Pool|Moonlit Pool]] collapsed at the moment of execution. [[Cora]] detected the party's volatile emotions across the tree line before they could engage -- their emotional state, still raw from the [[Queen of Darkness|Queen of Darkness's]] domain and the wine-blur exit in S30, was loud enough to read in a Feywild that treats emotion as a tactical signal. The DM summary does not record what shape the attempted attack took, only that it was useless.

Instead of fighting back, Cora **pitched a deal**: she had valuable information to give them, and she knew where [[Liora_Swiftclock|Liora]] was. The party went with her. Cora then **transported the party to the audience chamber of the [[Queen Meb|Queens of the Feywild]] -- [[Queen Meb|Meb]] and [[Queen Tiandra|Tiandra]]**, where she now sits in the room as a (suspect) intermediary.

### New Status: Tactical Hold
- Cora is now functioning as a channel between the party and the Queens' court.
- Killing her would also burn that channel and forfeit the deal she dangled (Liora's location, "valuable information").
- The S30 kill-plan is no longer the immediate path; the party is sitting on her offer instead.
- Whether this is a tactical truce, a co-option of the party, or both, is open.
- The lethal intent is not retracted -- Clover's *"She dies today"* and Modred's skull-goblet declaration are deferred, not cancelled.

### New Tactical Lessons
- **The Feywild reads emotion as a tactical signal at all times.** Cora detected the ambush across the tree line because the party was lit up like beacons to fae senses. Any future surprise on her requires controlling emotional state, not just position.
- **Cora will negotiate if caught unawares.** She did not fight back -- she pitched a deal. She can be made to talk if surprise is on the party's side.
- **Cora has access to the Queens.** She can transport the party directly into the Queens' court. This is new information about her reach.
