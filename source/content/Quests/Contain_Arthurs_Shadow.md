# Contain Arthur's Shadow

## Quick Reference
**Status:** Active (Queens of the Feywild have offered to seal the carrier inside a closed Feywild permanently -- party sitting on the offer; [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]] not present and did not consent)
**Priority:** CRITICAL
**Quest Giver:** The Icebreakers
**First Mentioned:** Session 22
**Last Updated:** Session 31

**Note:** This is a new tracking file as of Session 31, created to track the Shadow-containment thread that has now been raised explicitly by the Queens of the Feywild. Prior Shadow material was scattered across [[Stop_Tharizduns_Release]] and [[Personal/Jaspar_Prophetic_Dreams]]; this file consolidates the containment problem now that it has been named as a strategic option.

## Objective
Prevent the Shadow currently held by [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]] from ever being released to free [[Tharizdun]]. Decide on a containment plan that accounts for [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]] as a person, not just as a carrier.

## Background
The Shadow is **[[Arthur_De_Moray|Arthur De Moray]]'s Shadow** -- a corruption parasite tied to the Tharizdun cult. Since Session 22 / Session 27, it has been held by [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]]. The cult's interest in the Shadow is direct: under the campaign's stated mechanics, the Shadow can be used to release Tharizdun. Keeping it from the cult is therefore a Tharizdun-containment problem.

Until Session 31 the containment strategy was implicit -- keep Jaspar alive, keep the Shadow with him, don't let the cult get it. Jaspar's own Session 27 experiment (transferring corruption into a light battery) was framed as a possible containment device for the Shadow but remains unproven. See [[Stop_Tharizduns_Release]] Session 27 notes for the battery experiment.

Session 31 changed the strategic picture. The Queens of the Feywild named the Shadow-containment problem explicitly and offered a permanent solution.

## Session 31 - The Queens' Shadow Option
At the audience with [[Queen Meb|Meb]] and [[Queen Tiandra|Tiandra]] in the Feywild, the Queens raised a second strategic option alongside their main plan of sealing the Feywild (see [[Close_The_Feywild]]):

> **Whoever possesses the Shadow could be left in the Feywild so the Shadow would never be able to release Tharizdun.**

This is the first time in the campaign the party has been told that there is a containment strategy for the Shadow itself, not just for the cultist effort to use it. The mechanism is direct: if the Shadow's carrier remains inside the Feywild, and the Feywild is then sealed, the Shadow can never be released to free Tharizdun. The carrier is sealed inside with the realm.

The current carrier is [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]]. The proposal therefore targets him.

### What the DM Summary Does Not Say
- Whether the carrier would have to die for this to work, or live the rest of his life inside a sealed Feywild, or something else
- Whether the seal is reversible
- Whether the carrier's stay is voluntary
- Whether the carrier could be swapped (Shadow handed off to someone else who then stays)
- What happens to the Shadow itself inside a sealed Feywild

### Status: Offer on the Table
- The party has not yet responded.
- [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]] was not present at the Queens' court and did not consent.
- The party is now holding a moral problem the carrier did not get to weigh in on.
- The Shadow option is logically coupled to [[Close_The_Feywild]] -- it only works if the Feywild is sealed at all.

## Key Questions
- Does the carrier have to die for this to work, or live sealed inside the Feywild for the rest of his life?
- Is the seal reversible at any point?
- Can the Shadow be handed off to a different carrier before the seal closes?
- Does [[Jaspar_Starshade|Jaspar]] get a say -- and what does the party do if he says no?
- Is the [[Queen Meb|Queen of Darkness]]-side reading of this offer different from the [[Queen Tiandra|Seelie]] side? (One queen rules the court whose princess kidnapped Liora; the other gave the party a Sphinx-mark.)
