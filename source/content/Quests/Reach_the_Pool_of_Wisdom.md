# Reach the Pool of Wisdom

## Quick Reference
**Status:** Active (bypassed once via wine; longer-term fix still needed)
**Priority:** MEDIUM (infrastructure for all Feywild operations)
**Quest Giver:** [[Doggo]] (meadow-keeper golden retriever), reinforced by [[The Door]] in [[Mr. Plant]]'s basement
**Started:** Session 30
**Last Updated:** Session 30

## Objective
Find and visit the Pool of Wisdom (also called the Hot Springs of Wisdom) to bring the party's emotional state under control so they can travel the [[Feywild]] normally.

## Background
In the [[Feywild]], emotion is currency and emotion is fuel. Travel between places is **emotion-guided** -- a traveler visualizes a location or a person, and the Feywild moves them. This only works when the traveler's emotional state is checked. High, unchecked anxiety jams the system: the Feywild cannot move you while your head is full of thundering feelings.

The party arrived in the Feywild in Session 29 emotionally raw (New Sharandar had just burned, Liora had been taken, Jaspar was missing). The Sphinx Guardian read their emotions on sight as *"very high and all over the place."* Doggo, the golden retriever at the Meadow Tree, warned them explicitly:

> Their emotions were *"everywhere"* and would be used against them by fae if they did not get them checked.

He pointed them toward the **Pool of Wisdom** ("Hot Springs of Wisdom") as the fix. [[The Door]] in [[Mr. Plant]]'s basement confirmed the same thing: *"Traversing the Feywild is difficult without your emotions in check."*

Modred tried to emotion-travel from the Door and failed twice. His own cloud of emotions (Cora, the sky-eye, the detached hand, the Queen) formed into a thundercloud that screamed at him and bounced him back to the Door each time. The party is functionally grounded without the Pool.

## The Wine Workaround
At the end of Session 30, trapped in the [[Queen of Darkness]]'s diamond chamber with their anxiety weaponized against them, [[Sir_Modred_De_Moray|Modred]] pulled a bottle of wine and passed it around. Feywild intoxication is triple-speed. Drunk obliterated the party's anxiety just long enough that the realm moved them -- through many places at high speed -- and dumped them at the Moonlit Pool.

Wine is not a sustainable solution:
- It works by suppressing emotion, not regulating it
- The hangover was immediate and mild-debuffing
- Intoxication also sent them to wherever their **suppressed longing** wanted (Clover's childhood home) -- not a place they chose
- Future attempts are subject to the whim of the Feywild, not intentional travel
- No cleric means [[Calm Emotions]] is not a solution either

## What the Pool Does (from Doggo and The Door)
- Brings the party's emotional state under control
- Enables normal, intentional Feywild travel
- Possibly also blunts fae emotion-reading (so fae cannot exploit the party as easily)
- Exact rules unknown

## Where the Pool Is
According to Doggo and The Door, the Pool of Wisdom is **everywhere and nowhere** -- *"it finds you when you're ready."* It is not a fixed location. This is consistent with Feywild logic: the realm responds to internal state, not geography. The party does not travel **to** the Pool; the Pool appears **for** the party when they are in the right frame of mind.

This is circular -- you need emotional regulation to find the thing that gives you emotional regulation -- but the Feywild does not care.
