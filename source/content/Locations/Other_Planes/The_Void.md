# The Void

## Quick Reference
**Region:** Between Planes (between mortal realm and elsewhere)
**Type:** Extraplanar Space / The Afterlife
**Status:** Active, dangerous

## Description
A place of pure blackness accessed through triangular portals. The Void is the afterlife -- where all souls go when they die. Souls are supposed to be judged by the Boatman of the Void and sent to the higher planes or to the Nine Hells, but [[Tharizdun]] now controls the Boatman, causing all soul traffic to halt. No one reaches their final destination.

Those who enter feel the worst despair, loss, and nothingness. The Void separates travelers from each other and shows them personalized visions of their deepest fears and desires. False divine beings appear through portals similar to the entrance, attempting to lure travelers to "come with" them.

The Void also contains **chain locations** -- spaces where [[Tharizdun]]'s chains are anchored. These chains must be struck with the [[Gauntlets of Tyr]] to push Tharizdun further back into the Void (not free him). Tharizdun is bound by four chains going to different locations in the Void.

## Notable Areas

### Individual Vision Spaces
- Each traveler is separated and experiences their own nightmare
- The void creates personalized rooms/environments from the traveler's memories and fears
- False divine beings (beautiful winged figures) appear to offer help and salvation

### Chain Locations
- Spaces within the Void where [[Tharizdun]]'s chains are physically anchored
- A massive chain hangs from the air and ends at the floor
- Screams echo throughout the space
- The [[Gauntlets of Tyr]] must be used to strike these chains
- DM has confirmed these are no longer referred to as "Ruins" -- they are chain locations within the Void
- [[Lord Neverember]] recognized this space during Session 25: *"Is that a chain? It looks like this weird place that I went to."* -- confirming he was taken here during his possession
- First chain destroyed in Session 26 -- blue fire engulfed the chain and traveled upward to Tharizdun, setting his lower body ablaze
- After the first chain was struck, the space crumbled and the portal closed, trapping [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] and [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] temporarily

### Sabrina's Boat / The Afterlife Sea
- A vast expanse where millions of small boats carry dead souls with numb, blank faces
- All boats drift toward the [[Boatman of the Void]] -- a massive cloaked, floating entity
- [[Sabrina Nightgale]]'s boat is anchored here; she is tethered to it
- [[Clover]]'s parents are visible in the boat procession but unresponsive
- [[Tharizdun]] is visible in the distance, bound by four chains
- After Session 26: Tharizdun's lower body is on fire from the first chain's destruction
- The **Elemental Eye** -- a red eye -- has opened above Tharizdun's head, scanning downward

### Tharizdun's Realm
- Auren saw beyond the false divine to witness Tharizdun directly
- A massive demon bound in chains (originally described as 8, later clarified as 4)
- The mere sight burns the eyes
- Located deeper in the Void, seen when the deception fails
- After Session 26: lower body engulfed in blue fire, the Elemental Eye open above his head

## The Elemental Eye
- A red eye that opened above [[Tharizdun]]'s head after the first chain was destroyed in Session 26
- Scanned downward and fixated on the party's boat
- [[Sabrina Nightgale]]: *"The Elemental Eye has opened. Your friends don't have that much time."*
- Represents "the first phase of getting rid of Tharizdun" -- each chain destroyed has consequences and escalation
- Caused [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]]'s corruption to spread to his entire hand

## The Boatman of the Void
- A massive cloaked, floating entity that serves as the Judge of the dead
- Originally sorted souls to the higher planes or the Nine Hells
- Now controlled by [[Tharizdun]] -- all soul traffic is at a standstill
- [[Sabrina Nightgale]] theorized that killing the Boatman might free all trapped souls, including herself and [[Clover]]'s parents

## Portal Mechanics
- **Triangular portals** connect the material plane and the Void in both directions
- **From inside the Void:** draw a triangle (e.g., with a dagger) and say where you want to go to create a targeted portal to a specific destination
- **The boat's rudder/steering wheel** exits the Void, but to a random destination -- the Void chooses
- **Corruption risk:** entering and exiting the Void causes corruption effects (d100 roll)

## NPCs Found Here
- [[Sabrina Nightgale]] - Ghost tethered to her boat; provides lore and anti-corruption bread
- [[Boatman of the Void]] - Massive entity, Judge of the dead, controlled by Tharizdun
- False Divine Beings - Beautiful winged figures that attempt to deceive travelers
- [[Tharizdun]] - The Chained One, bound by four chains, lower body now on fire
- Clover's parents - Visible in the boat procession, unresponsive

## Events at This Location
- Session 7: Party entered through ritual portal. Each member experienced personalized nightmare visions:
  - Auren: Saw forest burning, Thistle's face melting, rejected false divine, witnessed Tharizdun in chains
  - Milo: Saw family frozen in ice, questioned false celestial until it flickered, fled in terror
  - Jaspar: Saw mother and himself as baby, learned he is Aasimar, asked for time to think
- Session 25: A triangular portal to the Void opened in [[The Depths]] when the Tharizdun corruption left [[Lord Neverember]]'s body. Through the portal, the party could see a chain location. Neverember recognized the chain space as somewhere he had been taken during his possession. The party chose not to enter immediately due to exhaustion.
- Session 26: [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] and [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] entered the Void through the portal. Modred used the [[Gauntlets of Tyr]] to strike the first chain -- blue fire engulfed the chain and climbed to Tharizdun, setting his lower body ablaze. The space crumbled and the portal closed, stranding them. They fell through the Void and landed on [[Sabrina Nightgale]]'s boat. The Elemental Eye opened above Tharizdun. Sabrina revealed the Void is the afterlife and explained the Boatman. She gave Modred anti-corruption bread and Milo a recipe. Milo drew a triangle with his dagger and said where he wanted to go, successfully creating a portal back to the [[Castellers Mansion|Castellers basement]]. Both Milo and Modred gained corruption effects upon return.
