# The Moonlit Pool

## Quick Reference
**Region:** [[Feywild]]
**Type:** Sacred druidic site / passage downward
**Status:** Active -- intact but dry, under [[House Castellers]] occupation

## Description
[[Clover]]'s childhood home in the Feywild -- a sacred druidic site he had believed destroyed by cultists years before he joined the party. **It is intact.** The pool itself is currently **dry**; the water has been drained. The site is surrounded by [[House Castellers]] tents and cages of captive fae creatures ("like the beginning of Shrek"), guarded by red-robed [[Tharizdun Cult|Tharizdun cultists]].

The pool functions as a **passage downward**. Jumping into the dry pool does not produce a fall -- the jumper simply vanishes, delivered "somewhere below" where a thing called **Okramok** is being protected.

This is the load-bearing revelation of Session 30 for Clover. His entire backstory -- that the Moonlit Pool was destroyed and his parents killed during a cultist attack -- is now retroactively in question. The pool exists, his parents are alive, and both were taken from him by a lie he has believed for years.

## Notable Areas

### The Dry Pool
- A ring where water should be, now empty
- Acts as a one-way gate downward when jumped into
- [[Clover]]'s parents leapt in on [[Cora]]'s command and vanished without hitting bottom
- Whatever is below is where "Okramok" is being sheltered

### The Castellers Camp
- Tents ringing the pool under [[House Castellers]] banner
- Cages of captured fey creatures of every kind
- Two cultists on perimeter patrol
- One cultist stationed directly beside [[Cora]]

### The Tree Line
- Where the party emerged, drunk-traveling out of the [[Queens Diamond Chamber|Queen's Diamond Chamber]]
- Hidden vantage on the camp at session end
- Approximately 100 feet from [[Cora]]

## NPCs Found Here
- [[Cora]] - Standing at the pool, back to the party, directing operations
- Clover's Parents - Older harengon druids, alive, last seen jumping into the dry pool on Cora's order
- House Castellers cultists - Three visible (one beside Cora, two on perimeter)
- Captive fae - Multiple, caged, identities unknown

## Items Found Here
- None recovered; the camp is still operational

## Events at This Location
- Session 30: Party emerged here from the [[Queens Diamond Chamber|Queen's Diamond Chamber]] via Feywild drunken emotion-travel. Witnessed [[Cora]] ordering Clover's parents to jump into the dry pool to protect "Okramok." Party ended the session hidden in the tree line, planning an ambush. Clover, on sight: *"I'm home."* Later: *"She dies today."*
- Session 31: Party revisited at session start, still concealed in the tree line. Watched [[Cora]] finish her conversation with Clover's parents; **Clover's parents jumped into the dry pool again and vanished below**. The party moved to attack -- the ambush they had spent the end of S30 planning -- but it was useless: [[Cora]] **read their emotions across the tree line** and detected them before they could engage. (S30's lesson about emotion-as-signal in the Feywild generalized: their emotional state was leaking to every fae in earshot.) Instead of fighting, Cora pitched a deal -- valuable information and [[Liora Swiftclock|Liora]]'s location -- and then **transported the party away from the Pool** to the [[Queens Court Feywild|Queens' Court]]. No combat occurred. The Castellers camp was not engaged.

## Secrets/Hidden Areas
- **Below the Pool:** Whatever is down there holds "Okramok." Clover's parents are now there. [[Cora]] directed the evacuation as she realized she had been followed into the Feywild.
- **What "Okramok" is:** Unknown. First mention of the name this session. Candidate for a Tharizdun-linked asset sheltered in the Feywild rather than the Material Plane.
- **Why the water is gone:** Unknown. Connected in some way to the Castellers operation on-site, or to opening the passage down.
- **Why Clover believed his parents died:** Unresolved. Backstory destabilized. Session 22's Void vision of his parents on a boat is now suspect.
- **Clover's parents' cooperation with Cora:** Coerced, deceived, compelled, or voluntary? They jumped on command without hesitation.
