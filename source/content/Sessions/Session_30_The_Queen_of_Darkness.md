# Session 30: The Queen of Darkness

**Date:** [[2026-04-22]]
**Party Present:** [[Milo Swiftclock]], [[Sir Modred De Moray]], [[Clover]]
**Absent:** [[Jaspar Starshade]] (Henrik not at table — Jaspar simply did not come through the portal tree with the rest of the party. Clover: *"Wait, was he tricking us this whole time?"*)

## Quick Summary

> The party stepped through the oak portal and immediately found themselves queued in a line of [[Tharizdun Cult|Tharizdun cultists]] waiting their turn at a [[Sphinx Guardian]], who swatted attacking cultists out of existence and marked each party member with a court sigil — [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] and [[Clover]] picked summer (Seelie golden flowers), [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] picked winter because "Waterdeep summer stinks like piss" and accidentally branded himself with the Unseelie black rose. A long, weird day in the [[Feywild]] followed: a golden retriever named [[Doggo]] bit off Modred's hand the instant he offered to "lend a hand," a flower-being called [[Mr. Plant]] sheltered them in his spiral garden and casually revealed that [[Cora]] is **the daughter of the Queen of the Unseelie Court**. After a long rest in Mr. Plant's cottage, the party descended into his basement and met [[The Door]] — a sentient, locked, offended fae door whose key has been stolen by a fairy. Climbing the stairs back up, they emerged not into the cottage but into the domain of the **[[Queen of Darkness]]**, a massive black diamond bleeding fog who offered them their lost loved ones as shadow-puppets. Milo caught his shadow-[[Liora Swiftclock|Liora]] lying; Modred caught his shadow-[[Arthur De Moray|Arthur]] lying; but Clover's parents — who he has believed dead for years — answered his age-4 spell test *word for word*. **They are alive.** The party escaped the Queen by getting drunk on Feywild wine at triple speed, and landed through a travel-blur at the [[The Moonlit Pool|Moonlit Pool]] — Clover's intact childhood home, dry, surrounded by [[House Castellers]] tents and caged fae. From the tree line they watched Cora order Clover's parents to jump into the dry pool to protect a brand-new proper noun: **Okramok**. They vanished into it. Session ended on an ambush setup, with Clover whispering *"She dies today."*

## Key Events Timeline

1. **Through the oak** — The party stepped through the hole in the oak portal tree from the Session 29 cliffhanger. Unlike every other portal they've used, this one felt like just walking through a door. They emerged into a narrow, cold stone corridor, lined up automatically behind a handful of red-robed [[Tharizdun Cult|Tharizdun cultists]]. A female voice ahead said *"Next."* Jaspar was not with them.
2. **The Sphinx Guardian** — The [[Sphinx Guardian]] processed the cultists one by one, swatting each out of existence when they drew a blade. At the front of the line, she asked the party how they felt and whether they preferred summer or winter. Milo picked summer — golden flower on his chest. Clover picked summer, hopeful — golden flower on his armor. Modred picked winter because "Waterdeep summers stink like piss" — **black rose burned into his armor, the Unseelie mark**.
3. **Doggo and the lost hand** — Through a sunlit meadow with broken time, the party met [[Doggo]], a golden retriever under a tree. Doggo asked Modred to *"lend me a hand, please."* Modred offered. His hand came off his wrist in Doggo's mouth. Doggo spat it back, flower-covered. Modred reattached it. No permanent damage.
4. **Clover's Feywild 101** — Clover explained: *"Whenever anybody says anything to you here, they mean it in the most literal sense. When someone asks you to lend them a hand — it's your hand."* He admitted this was his first time back in the Feywild since he left.
5. **Mage Hand (first time ever)** — Milo solved Doggo's bone problem by casting **Mage Hand for the first time in his entire adventuring career** — the table cheered. The hand went up into the tree hollow, spooked the rabbit, dropped the bone. The rabbit deep-voiced *"What the fuck is happening?"* and vanished.
6. **Doggo's gift** — Modred gained a new ability: while in the Feywild, he can cast **Mage Hand once per short rest**. He immediately detached his hand as a party trick.
7. **Mr. Plant's spiral** — The party reached a large spiral flower bed with a cottage at its center. [[Mr. Plant]], a tall Bellsprout-like flower being, was watering the plants. Modred tickled a flower; it cried; Mr. Plant scolded him. One of his head-flowers whistled open and **vomited a dead cultist** onto the ground as a friendliness demonstration.
8. **The load-bearing reveal** — Modred asked Mr. Plant about Cora. Mr. Plant's response: *"Are you talking about the daughter of the Unseelie?"* **[[Cora]] is the daughter of the ruler of the Unseelie Court — the [[Queen of Darkness|Queen of the Winter Court]].** Modred, who had just accidentally chosen the Unseelie mark, took the news poorly. Clover sighed: *"You chose the Unseelie. Well — what's done is done."*
9. **The Eye** — When Modred tried to argue his choice didn't mean anything, a giant disembodied eye opened in the sky above him and fixed on him. *"I'm having my eye on you,"* said Mr. Plant, literally.
10. **Name games** — Clover asked Mr. Plant for his name. *"Mr. Plant."* He asked Modred. *"Black."* Modred's nat-20 Insight confirmed both names were fake — a fae giving a stranger their real name is suspicious in itself. Later Modred fed Mr. Plant a false name for Liora: *"Lyra Swiftclaw."*
11. **Unsuccessful rose cover-up** — Modred tried to hide the Unseelie black rose with a cape toga; the rose bled through the cloth with its outline intact. It cannot be hidden.
12. **Long rest in the cottage** — Mr. Plant offered shelter and a mandatory basement visit. The party accepted. Inside: hanging vines, windows showing night while outside was day, a microscopic civilization in the blanket fabric, a dead cultist hanging upside down dripping feed into flowers. Milo's Detect Magic pinged everything. Modred snored in chorus with the whole flower household. They rested. Vines grew visibly; the cultist corpse had bloomed into flowers; Mr. Plant was gone; his eye still watched.
13. **The Door** — Downstairs, a bridge over peaceful ocean led to a single wooden door set in open air. When Clover reached for the handle, it turned into a human hand and slapped his away. *"How dare you touch me?"* [[The Door]] was sentient, fussy, and could transport them anywhere in the Feywild by name — but was **locked**. A fairy had stolen its key. It requested duck oil as a future gift.
14. **Anxiety-cloud bouncing** — The Door explained Feywild travel runs on emotion and the fix is the [[Pool of Wisdom]]. Modred tried twice to visualize the Pool and travel there. Both times his own anxiety-cloud — Cora, the eye, the detached hand — formed into a screaming thunderhead and bounced him back to the Door. The Door was audibly amused.
15. **Back up the stairs — into the wrong room** — The party climbed the basement stairs back up. They did not come out into the cottage. They came out into a dark chamber centered on **a massive black diamond floating in midair, bleeding black fog**. A woman's voice greeted them: *"Welcome to my domain, travelers. I see your emotions so vividly, so fresh... so delicious."* The [[Queen of Darkness]]. The ruler of the Unseelie. Cora's mother.
16. **The shadow-puppets** — The Queen peeled three figures from her fog:
    - **Shadow-[[Arthur De Moray|Arthur]]** reached for Modred: *"Brother, come to me. The place is burning. Why haven't you come?"* Modred asked his favorite dish. Shadow-Arthur didn't know. The Queen laughed: *"Very clever of you."* The shadow did not dissipate.
    - **Shadow-[[Liora Swiftclock|Liora]]** reached for Milo: *"They have captured me. Please, you need to find me."* Milo asked who she'd spoken with outside their house. The shadow glitched mid-answer. The Queen: *"You are very smart for such a tiny creature."*
    - **Shadow-[[Clover]]'s parents**. Both. Hardened from fog into a man and a woman. *"Clover, you need to save us. We're at the [[The Moonlit Pool|Moonlit Pool]] right now."* Clover asked them to repeat the first spell he cast at age 4. **They answered it back word for word. They were real.** *"We are real, but these are not. We are alive. Leave this place and just — try to find us."* Clover's jaw was on the floor. He has believed his parents died in the cultist attack on the Moonlit Pool for years.
17. **The Queen's offer refused** — The Queen offered to transport the party to their loved ones herself, at no cost. Clover rolled a nat-20 Insight and read the offer as pure malevolence. He refused. *"Well, then you will never find peace. You will never find your family, even though they are alive and in my care."*
18. **Failed meditation escape** — The party tried 4-7-8 breathing. Two rounds of pooled Wisdom saves (Milo 13/14, Clover 22/19, Modred 18/13). They got glimpses of places to travel — but the Queen amplified their emotions and tore them back into the diamond chamber each time.
19. **The wine solution** — Modred remembered he had a bottle of wine. He took a pull. Everyone drank. **Feywild intoxication hits triple speed.** Drunkenness flattened anxiety. The Queen's grip slipped. The chamber crashed open, and the party tumbled through a blurred montage of places they'd visited — the Door, the Sphinx (who looked confused: *"How are you even here?"*), many more — before the drunk wore off into hangover and dropped them outside a tree line.
20. **"I'm home."** — Without rolling anything, Clover recognized the place. **The [[The Moonlit Pool|Moonlit Pool]]. His actual childhood home. Intact.** Not destroyed by cultists as his backstory held. It was here. It had just been lost to him.
21. **The camp at the Pool** — Through the trees: the Moonlit Pool itself, **dry**. [[House Castellers]] tents. Cages of captured fae creatures. Two cultists on perimeter, one beside [[Cora]], and Cora standing at the dry pool edge speaking with two older harengons — **Clover's parents. Alive. The same ones the Queen's shadow-puppets had copied minutes ago.**
22. **Okramok** — Clover read Cora's lips from the tree line. Cora: *"I've been followed in here. We need to protect **Okramok**. You need to jump down into the pool now."* Without hesitation, Clover's parents jumped into the dry pool and vanished. Cora turned to look at the caged fae. **Okramok is a brand-new proper noun the party has never heard before.**
23. **Ambush setup** — Clover pointed Cora out to Milo and Modred: *"She dies today."* The party started sketching a plan. Clover tentatively proposed turning into a giant constrictor snake and choking Cora out. Modred volunteered to take the three cultists. Milo proposed at least three fireballs. Clover gently asked them not to burn down his childhood home. Modred: *"If we kill her, I'm gonna make a goblet from her skull."* Session closed on the plan in progress.

## Revelations & Lore

- **The Feywild runs on fae literalism.** Every word said to a fae is taken at its strictest meaning. *"Lend me a hand"* means your hand. Asking for someone's name takes the actual name. Giving your name voluntarily is giving power away. Clover, Feywild-native, knows this; the Material-Plane PCs are learning it the hard way.
- **Emotion is the Feywild's fuel and currency.** Fae can see emotion directly and taste it. Travel runs on emotional state — high, unchecked anxiety makes you untravelable. The fix is the [[Pool of Wisdom]] ("Hot Springs of Wisdom"), which "finds you when you're ready."
- **Wine breaks anxiety hard enough to travel.** Intoxication in the Feywild is triple-speed. Getting drunk flattens emotional noise long enough that the realm will move you — and dumps you wherever your suppressed longing actually wanted to go.
- **The Sphinx's seasonal choice is a court binding.** Summer = Seelie Court mark (golden flower). Winter = Unseelie Court mark (black rose). The mark imprints on body or armor and cannot be hidden — it bleeds through anything laid over it, outline and all. Creatures in the Feywild read the mark on sight.
- **[[Cora]] is the daughter of the Queen of the Unseelie Court** — "the [[Queen of Darkness]]," Queen of the Winter Court. The party has been fighting the crown princess of the Winter Court this whole time. This recontextualizes her triangular portals, her flash-teleport, and her ability to move between the Material Plane and the Feywild.
- **The Queen of Darkness presents as a floating black diamond** bleeding black fog. Clover recognized her from childhood scare-tales. She can manifest shadow-figures from the fog that speak with other people's voices and mimic emotional presence — but **she cannot fake specific private facts** (favorite dish, spell-at-age-4).
- **[[Clover]]'s parents are ALIVE.** They are at the [[The Moonlit Pool|Moonlit Pool]] in the Feywild, with [[Cora]]. They confirmed their reality with Clover's age-4 spell. **This directly contradicts Clover's backstory**, which held that his parents were killed in the cultist attack on the Moonlit Pool — an event he witnessed and has been grieving for 29 sessions. The Moonlit Pool was *not* destroyed; it is intact, though dry. His Session 22 Void-vision of his parents on a boat in the underworld is now suspect.
- **[[House Castellers]] has a presence in the Feywild at the Moonlit Pool.** Tents and cages of captive fae surround the dry pool. The cult is running a fae-captivity operation on-site, guarded by cultists. This is a new operational capability.
- **The Moonlit Pool can be physically entered.** It is dry. Cora ordered Clover's parents to jump in, and they did — they vanished without hitting bottom. It functions as a passage to somewhere further in. **Okramok** is below.
- **Okramok** — brand-new proper noun. Something [[Cora]] and Clover's parents are "protecting" beneath the dry Moonlit Pool. No description, no reference, no pronoun. Could be an object, person, god-fragment, or location. First mention this session. Plot-critical.
- **A fairy has stolen [[The Door]]'s key.** Finding the key (or the fairy) would open an everywhere-to-everywhere travel node in the Feywild. Duck oil is an alternative tribute.
- **Detect Magic in the Feywild is useless** — it pings enchantment on literally everything. Milo got a headache.
