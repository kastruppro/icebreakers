# Session 26: The First Chain

**Date:** [[2026-03-11]]
**Party Present:** [[Milo Swiftclock]], [[Sir Modred De Moray]], [[Jaspar Starshade]], [[Clover]] (played by DM as NPC)

## Quick Summary
> Picking up immediately after Session 25, [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] and [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] entered [[The Void]] through the triangular portal and struck the first of [[Tharizdun]]'s chains with the [[Gauntlets of Tyr]], engulfing the chained god's lower body in blue fire. The act opened a terrifying red eye — the Elemental Eye — above Tharizdun's skull. The portal collapsed, trapping them in the Void where [[Sabrina Nightgale]] revealed that the Void is the afterlife itself, controlled by Tharizdun through a corrupted entity called the Boatman. Milo drew a triangle with his dagger to portal the pair back to the material plane, where they reunited with [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] — who had received a divine visitation reiterating the deal to destroy the Shadow. [[Liora Swiftclock|Liora]]'s desperate telepathic messages revealed the Swiftclock family is hiding from [[Cora]] in [[New Sharandar]]. After resting at the [[Temple of Tyr]], the party visited a reformed [[Sir Goodwin De Moray]], who provided gold, a [[Sending Stone]], and intelligence about cult activity in [[Neverwinter Forest]]. The session ended with the party preparing to leave [[Waterdeep]] for [[Neverwinter]], with multiple urgent threads converging.

14. **Visit to House De Moray** - [[Sir Goodwin De Moray|Goodwin]] apologized, gave 350 gold and a [[Sending Stone]], confirmed cult activity in [[Neverwinter Forest]], and shared his account of Arthur's Shadow possession at a cult ruin.

## Revelations & Lore

- **The Elemental Eye:** When the party destroyed the first chain, a red eye called the Elemental Eye opened above [[Tharizdun]]'s head — the first phase of getting rid of him. Each chain destroyed likely causes further escalation.
- **The Void Is the Afterlife:** The Void is where all dead souls go. They are supposed to be judged and sent to the higher planes or to the Nine Hells.
- **The Boatman of the Void:** A massive cloaked, floating entity that serves as the Judge of the dead. [[Tharizdun]] now controls it, causing all soul traffic to halt. Killing the Boatman might free all trapped souls, including [[Sabrina Nightgale|Sabrina]] and [[Clover]]'s parents.
- **Sabrina's Party Killed 3 of 4 Princes:** They failed on the fourth. [[Edward Hamel]], the last survivor, is buried in [[Waterdeep]] and may have left knowledge or items at his house.
- **Prince Dungeons Move:** The ruins containing Princes change locations over time. Cult activity near a location signals a nearby dungeon.
- **Void Portal Mechanics:** From inside the Void, you can draw a triangle and specify a destination to create a targeted portal. The boat's rudder exits the Void to a random location.
- **Sabrina's Bread Recipe:** An anti-corruption bread made with [[Waterdeep]] ingredients and forest elf magic. Contains corruption but does not cure it.
- **The Shadow in Neverwinter Forest:** The [[Divine Entity]] confirmed the Shadow (possessing Arthur De Moray) is roaming [[Neverwinter Forest]] in pain, chasing something. People are in danger.
- **Jaspar Was Born to Destroy the Shadow:** The entity stated Jaspar is "a product of this divine being" and was born specifically to contain and end the Shadow. The Shadow must be dealt with in the Void, not the material plane.
- **Goodwin's Account of Arthur's Possession:** [[Sir Goodwin De Moray|Goodwin]] and Arthur went to a cult ruin in the forest outside [[Waterdeep]]. During a fight with cultists performing a ritual, an explosion occurred and something shadowy flew into Arthur. No healer could fix it afterward.
- **Cult Activity in Neverwinter Forest:** Confirmed by both [[Sir Goodwin De Moray|Goodwin]]'s spies and [[Liora Swiftclock|Liora]]'s messages. Likely signals another Prince dungeon location.
- **Corruption Escalation:** Both [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] and [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] gained corruption effects. Milo: +5 permanent HP, blackened cheek fur, 12-hour temp HP ban. Modred: Corrupted Second Wind ability (double healing, d100 risk), entire hand now black.
