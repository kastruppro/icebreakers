## Quick Summary

> The party tried to execute the ambush on [[Cora]] they had been planning at the end of Session 30 — and it fell apart at the threshold. Cora's parents jumped back into the dry [[The Moonlit Pool|Moonlit Pool]], and before the party could engage, Cora sensed them through the tree line: their emotions, still raw from the [[Queen of Darkness]]'s domain and the wine-blur exit, were lighting them up like beacons for any fae nearby. Instead of fighting, Cora pitched a deal — valuable information, plus the location of [[Liora Swiftclock|Milo's sister Liora]] — in exchange for the party coming with her. They went. She transported them to the audience chamber of the two Queens of the [[Feywild]]: **[[Queen Meb|Meb]]** and **[[Queen Tiandra|Tiandra]]**, rumored former enemies, now allied against the [[Tharizdun Cult]]. The Queens laid out a plan: seal the Feywild against external access so [[Tharizdun]] cannot reach it after his release. For this they need [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] specifically. They also floated a second, harder option — leave whoever carries the Shadow inside the sealed Feywild forever, so the Shadow can never be used to free Tharizdun. That carrier is [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]], who was not present to hear any of it. No decision was given. No combat occurred. The session closed with "a lot to think about."

## Revelations & Lore

- **There are two Queens of the [[Feywild]], [[Queen Meb|Meb]] and [[Queen Tiandra|Tiandra]], and they are now allied.** Per rumor they were previously at war. The alliance is built around a shared enemy — the [[Tharizdun Cult]] — and around a shared goal: closing the Feywild against external access.
- **Strong working inference (NOT confirmed by the DM summary):** Meb is likely the [[Queen of Darkness]] from S30 — Cora's mother, ruler of the Unseelie / Winter Court. Tiandra is likely the Seelie / Summer Queen. If true, the party is now standing in front of (a) the same entity that tried to anchor them to her diamond chamber last session, now in audience-form, and (b) the highest authority of the Seelie Court they took a flower mark for at the Sphinx Guardian. **This identification is unconfirmed and is the single most important thing to clear up next session.**
- **The [[Tharizdun Cult|Cult]] is operating inside the Feywild at sustained scale.** The Queens' framing was "destroying everything," not "raiding occasionally." The S30 observations (cultists at the Sphinx, cultist compost at Mr. Plant's, the Castellers camp with cages of captured fey at the Moonlit Pool) are a sample of a wider problem.
- **The Queens' strategic plan is to seal the Feywild.** Closing the realm against outside access would secure it against Tharizdun's reach after his release. The Queens are taking Tharizdun's release seriously enough to consider isolating their realm from the multiverse.
- **[[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] is specifically required** for the sealing plan. The Queens did not ask the party collectively — they asked Milo. The summary does not say why.
- **The Shadow can be neutralized by leaving its carrier inside a sealed Feywild.** This is the first explicit containment plan in the campaign for the Shadow itself, rather than for the cult's effort to wield it. It targets the carrier — currently [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] — who was not present to consent.
- **[[Cora]] is willing to negotiate when caught.** Faced with a botched ambush, she did not fight; she pitched a deal and brokered an audience. She has access to the Queens. Her role this session is intermediary, which contradicts the S30 read of her as hostile princess and needs reconciling.
- **The Feywild reads emotion as tactical signal at all times** — not just inside the [[Queen of Darkness]]'s domain. Cora detected the ambush off the party's ragged emotional state through the tree line. The S30 lesson (anxiety jams Feywild travel) generalizes: in the Feywild, the party's emotional state is information leaking to every fae in earshot, including hostile ones.
