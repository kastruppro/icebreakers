# Session 27: The Corruption Experiment

**Date:** [[2026-03-21]]
**Party Present:** [[Milo Swiftclock]], [[Sir Modred De Moray]], [[Jaspar Starshade]], [[Clover]] (played by DM as NPC)

## Quick Summary
> [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] conducted a reckless experiment at the [[Sated Satyr]] inn, transferring [[Tharizdun]]'s purple corruption substance into his light battery. The experiment gave him 4 corrupted spells and turned both arms black to the shoulders — but also froze the entire inn in a time stop that [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] had to break using his chronomancy. [[Lord Neverember]] revealed the cursed crown from Neverwinter's crypt was intentionally cursed and slowly kills its wearer. After scouting [[Edward Hamel]]'s grave at the [[Waterdeep Cemetery]] and learning new spells, the party used Jaspar's reconfigured gauntlet to open a triangular portal directly to [[Neverwinter]] — bypassing the Void entirely. Upon arrival, they discovered [[Sebastian]]'s control runs far deeper than expected: mass memory enchantment has rewritten the populace's minds, and the guards genuinely believe Sebastian has always been their lord. [[Lord Neverember]] was not recognized at his own gates. The party now walks disguised through propaganda-filled streets toward the [[Thirsty Goat]] tavern, hoping Jaspar's contacts in the docks have escaped Sebastian's spell.

## Key Events Timeline

1. **Jaspar's Corruption Battery Experiment** - [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] transferred [[Tharizdun]]'s purple substance into his light battery at the [[Sated Satyr]]. The corruption mixed with his light energy, triggering a vision of [[Tharizdun]] and granting 4 corrupted spells (Cure Wounds, Fire Bolt, Faerie Fire, Mending). His arms turned black to the shoulders and he collapsed, accidentally freezing the entire inn in a time stop.
2. **Milo's Beacon Watch Reacts** - [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]]'s beacon watch grew burning hot with hands spinning wildly, detecting chronomancy disturbances. [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] used the [[Gauntlets of Tyr]] to safely hold it and swept it around — the watch fixated toward the inn.
3. **The Frozen Inn** - [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] and [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] entered the [[Sated Satyr]] to find every patron frozen in time. Pressure in their ears, slow movement, electrical atmosphere. [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]]'s Arcana check (17) confirmed chronomancy — similar to the mountain encounter with [[Cora]].
4. **Milo Breaks the Time Stop** - Upstairs, they found [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] unconscious with energy pulsing between him and the battery. [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] touched the battery and saw an apocalyptic vision — fields burning, meteors falling, a demon's face in the sky. [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] used Chronal Shift to crush the temporal walls and restore normal time.
5. **The Shadow Containment Debate** - The party argued about Jaspar's recklessness and debated whether his battery could absorb the Shadow from [[Arthur De Moray]]. Jaspar defended it as "proof of concept." [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] worried about the technology falling into the wrong hands. Jaspar countered: "The alternative is what? Killing your brother?"
6. **Cursed Crown Revelation** - [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] showed [[Lord Neverember]] the cursed crown from the Neverwinter Castle crypt. Neverember confirmed it was intentionally cursed to prevent theft: "You would feel very powerful, but you would also die slow."
7. **Preparations in Waterdeep** - [[Milo Swiftclock|Milo]] learned [[Pulse Wave]] and [[Fog Cloud]]. [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] reconfigured his gauntlet's 3 lenses into a triangular alignment for portal-opening. [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] sharpened his blade and watched skeptically.
8. **Allies Discussion with Neverember** - [[Lord Neverember]] mentioned [[House Timore]] could lend soldiers and [[Liset]] (the masked woman with the floating bar) is a useful intelligence asset. The party discussed portal vs. boat travel.
9. **Raenar and Neverember's Argument** - At [[Raenar Neverember|Raenar]]'s place, the party overheard father and son arguing about Neverember's absence during Raenar's childhood. Raenar put on a fake smile when the party arrived. [[Sir Modred De Moray|Modred]] reminded Neverember: "He missed you very much."
10. **Cemetery Scouting** - The party scouted [[Edward Hamel]]'s grave at the [[Waterdeep Cemetery]]. Guard patrols made digging impractical — postponed for a future visit.
11. **Portal to Neverwinter** - [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] used his reconfigured gauntlet to open a triangular portal using 3 points of light instead of a knife. The portal bypassed the Void entirely — direct material-plane-to-material-plane travel. Everyone stepped through with pain but no Void passage. Jaspar's corruption advanced further (d100: 71, +2 saving throw bonus). [[Lord Neverember]] and [[Raenar Neverember|Raenar]] screamed in pain but showed no visible corruption.
12. **Sebastian's Memory Enchantment Revealed** - At [[Neverwinter]]'s gates, guards did not recognize [[Lord Neverember]]. A guard stated Sebastian has been lord "as long as I've been born." The populace's memories have been magically rewritten. Banners with Sebastian's face and an eye symbol hung throughout the city.
13. **Disguised Entry to Neverwinter** - The party donned cloaks and hoods, entered as "merchants," and headed toward the [[Thirsty Goat]] tavern in the docks — where [[Sebastian]]'s influence is likely weakest.
14. **Jaspar's Docks Proposal** - While walking, [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] pitched [[Lord Neverember]] on giving him control of the [[Neverwinter]] docks as a reward. Neverember seemed cautiously interested before the session ended.

## Revelations & Lore

- **Cursed Crown of Neverwinter:** [[Lord Neverember]] confirmed the crown from the crypt was cursed intentionally by the guardians to prevent theft. If worn, it grants a feeling of power but slowly kills the wearer. The guardians only gave it away because something was very wrong.
- **Sebastian's Memory Magic:** [[Sebastian]] has cast some form of mass enchantment on [[Neverwinter]]'s populace — guards (and presumably citizens) genuinely believe Sebastian has always been their lord. This is deeper than simple mind control; memories appear to have been rewritten.
- **Jaspar's Corruption Battery:** The purple substance ([[Tharizdun]]'s energy) can be contained in [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]]'s light battery, mixing with his own energy. The two forces fight but coexist. However, the containment causes massive corruption to the handler and time-stopping side effects in the surrounding area.
- **Portal Without the Void:** [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]]'s gauntlet-based portal method (using 3 lenses in triangular configuration with the corrupted battery) allowed direct material-plane-to-material-plane travel without passing through the Void. Faster to create and more precise, though still causes pain and corruption.
- **Corruption Spells Mechanic:** [[Jaspar Starshade|Jaspar]] now has 4 corrupted spells (Cure Wounds, Fire Bolt, Faerie Fire, Mending). For each, he can choose to cast the normal or corrupted version. Corrupted versions require a d100 roll with unpredictable effects.
- **House Timore as Potential Ally:** [[Lord Neverember]] mentioned [[House Timore]] could lend soldiers for retaking [[Neverwinter]].
- **The Masked Woman ([[Liset]]):** The opposition figure with the floating bar was mentioned as a potential intelligence ally — she is clever and knows a lot about the city, though not a fighter.
